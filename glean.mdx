Glea is Mintlify's most strategically compelling expansion target. They are an enterprise AI search and knowledge platform valued at 7.2 Billion with 200 Million ARR, doubling revenue in nine months. They are also a committed, expanding Mintlify customer — making them the highest-priority account for a targeted AI Assistant conversation.

---

## What Stands Out About Their Mintlify Usage

Glean runs a genuinely sophisticated Mintlify deployment. Their developer portal auto-generates API reference documentation directly from OpenAPI specs — covering a Client API (chat, search, content discovery), an Indexing API (custom data sources), and an Agents API (enterprise AI agent building). Official SDK docs for Python, TypeScript, Go, and Java sit alongside structured quickstarts, authentication guides, and a dedicated Docs MCP Server section. The site is heavily branded with custom CSS that precisely matches Glean's visual identity; they've invested in the presentation layer, not just the content.

Before Mintlify, Glean's documentation was fragmented across multiple systems: help center content in **Intercom**, support workflows in Zendesk, and developer guides in custom markdown. They consolidated the documentation structure and publishing layer onto Mintlify — but Intercom appears to remain active as their AI support agent across both the help center and developer docs, with Fin still fielding user queries on top of the Mintlify-hosted content. In other words, Glean made Mintlify their documentation platform while keeping Intercom in the support and chat layer.

<Note>
  What matters most right now: Glean has since expanded to a **second Mintlify property**. Their help center (docs.glean.com) and their developer portal are both running on the platform. This is not passive usage — it is an organization that has explicitly chosen to deepen its investment in Mintlify twice. Recent expansion behavior is the strongest available predictor of future expansion behavior, and it means an internal champion has already made the case for Mintlify investment more than once.
</Note>

## Expansion Opportunity: Mintlify AI Assistant

The expansion opportunity is Mintlify's **AI Assistant** — specifically the agentic retrieval model with source citations — deployed across both Mintlify properties.

Glean's developer docs span multiple APIs, authentication variants, SDK quickstarts, agent-building patterns, and MCP configuration. Developers landing on these pages ask specific, high-stakes technical questions in the moment: *"How do I handle streaming in the Chat API?"* or *"What is the token scope for Indexing?"* These are exactly the queries an AI Assistant with deep contextual retrieval resolves in seconds — without a support ticket or a Slack ping to a developer advocate.

Glean ships **200+ features per fiscal year**. Every release cycle adds documentation surface area. More complexity means more developer questions. A deployed AI Assistant on both Mintlify properties would deflect a growing share of that inbound noise, freeing the developer relations team for high-value work rather than answering the same questions on repeat.

This is also a natural use-case expansion play. Glean is already running two distinct documentation surfaces (developer portal and help center). Activating the AI Assistant on both, at volume, creates a meaningful step up in AI credit consumption — which is Mintlify's primary organic expansion lever.

---

## Why Haven't They Adopted It Yet?

<Warning>
  The most likely blocker is **Intercom Fin**. Intercom's AI agent appears to still be actively deployed on both Glean documentation properties, fielding user queries on top of their Mintlify content. This is not a question of feature awareness or adoption friction — it is a **vendor displacement conversation**.
</Warning>

Fin is already in place, someone internally owns and renews that Intercom contract, and they have an established relationship with the vendor. That internal Intercom champion is the likely source of resistance. They may not even be aware of Mintlify's native AI Assistant as an alternative — but if they are, they have structural incentives to protect the Intercom relationship: existing workflows, historical data, and a vendor contract that may have meaningful remaining term. Displacing an incumbent is a different motion than introducing a net-new tool, and it requires a more deliberate approach to the internal champion map.

A secondary dynamic is brand tension. Glean sells enterprise AI search and knowledge retrieval. There is an implicit awkwardness in running a third-party AI agent on their own documentation rather than their own product — but this is a weaker lever than the displacement question and unlikely to drive a decision on its own.

## What You'd Need to Learn to Qualify

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Who owns the Intercom relationship, and are they the same person managing Mintlify?">
    If the Intercom contract and the Mintlify account sit with the same owner, there is an opening to consolidate — they already understand the trade-off and have the authority to make a switch. If they are siloed across different teams, the Mintlify champion will have limited visibility into the Intercom contract and less leverage to drive displacement. Mapping this ownership gap is the first qualification step before any expansion conversation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How fast is their developer community growing?">
    Glean doubled ARR to $200M in nine months. Their external developer ecosystem is growing at pace. More developers means more documentation traffic and more queries hitting Fin. If Fin's resolution rate is declining or support escalations are rising, that creates a natural opening to introduce Mintlify's AI Assistant as a purpose-built alternative — grounded in the documentation itself rather than a generalized support agent.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

The strongest signal is **already present**: Glean recently added a second Mintlify property. Someone internally championed that decision, secured budget, and executed. That champion exists, has credibility, and is already comfortable making the case for Mintlify investment. This is where to start.
